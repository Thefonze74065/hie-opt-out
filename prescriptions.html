Prescription Medication Privacy

Protect your prescription data from sharing through pharmacy networks and insurance systems

Every time you fill a prescription or schedule an appointment, your medication history and scheduling data may be shared through networks like SureScripts, connecting pharmacies, insurance companies, and healthcare providers across the country.

Major Prescription Data Networks

SureScripts

The largest prescription network in the US, connecting over 70,000 pharmacies and 2.5 million healthcare providers. Also handles appointment scheduling data.

NCPDP

National Council for Prescription Drug Programs - sets standards for prescription data exchange.

RxHub

Owned by CVS Health, connecting pharmacies with healthcare providers for prescription history sharing.

What Data is Being Shared

  • Prescription History: All medications you've filled, including dates and quantities
  • Controlled Substances: Pain medications, mental health drugs, and other regulated prescriptions
  • Appointment Scheduling: Doctor appointment dates, times, and types of visits
  • Pharmacy Information: Which pharmacies you use and when
  • Insurance Claims: What your insurance covered and what you paid out-of-pocket
  • Adherence Data: Whether you're taking medications as prescribed
  • Drug Interactions: Analysis of your complete medication profile

Who Has Access

  • Healthcare Providers: Doctors can see your prescription history from any pharmacy
  • Insurance Companies: Track your medication use for coverage decisions
  • Pharmacy Chains: Share data across all their locations
  • Government Agencies: DEA, state monitoring programs for controlled substances
  • Research Organizations: De-identified data for pharmaceutical research
  • Third-Party Vendors: Companies providing services to pharmacies and insurers

Step-by-Step Opt-Out Process

1

Contact Your Pharmacy

Call or visit each pharmacy you use and ask to speak with the pharmacy manager or privacy officer.

What to say: "I want to opt out of all prescription data sharing through SureScripts, RxHub, and any other networks. I do not consent to sharing my prescription history with other healthcare providers or organizations."
2

Contact Your Insurance

Call your health insurance company and ask about prescription data sharing policies.

What to ask: "Do you share my prescription claims data with third parties? How can I opt out of prescription data sharing for care coordination?"
3

Request Written Confirmation

Ask each pharmacy and your insurance company for written confirmation of your opt-out preferences.

Documentation: Keep copies of all opt-out forms and confirmation letters.
4

Follow Up Regularly

Check your opt-out status annually, as policies and systems may change.

Timeline: Follow up every 6-12 months to ensure your preferences are still in effect.

Important Limitations & Considerations

What You CAN Control

  • Sharing prescription history for "care coordination"
  • Marketing and research uses of your data
  • Some third-party access to your prescription records
  • Cross-pharmacy sharing of non-controlled substances

What You CANNOT Control

  • Insurance claims processing and fraud prevention
  • DEA monitoring of controlled substances
  • State prescription drug monitoring programs (PDMPs)
  • Pharmacy benefit manager (PBM) access for coverage decisions
  • Legal requirements for sharing in emergencies
