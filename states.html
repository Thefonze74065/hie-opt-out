General Guidance for All States

Even if your state isn't listed above, you may still have opt-out rights through federal laws and provider policies:

Steps to Try:

  1. Contact your healthcare provider's Privacy Officer (listed on HIPAA notices)
  2. Ask specifically about "Health Information Exchange opt-out procedures"
  3. Mention Epic Care Everywhere, CareQuality, or other HIE networks by name
  4. Request written confirmation of any opt-out process
  5. Follow up if you don't receive a response within 30 days

Help us expand: If you successfully opt out in a state not listed here, please consider sharing your experience to help others in your state.

Understanding State vs. Provider Opt-Outs

State-Level HIE

Many states operate their own health information exchanges:

  • North Carolina: NC HealthConnex
  • New York: SHIN-NY (Statewide HIE)
  • California: Various regional HIEs
  • Texas: Texas HIE Cooperative

These require separate opt-out processes from provider networks.

Provider-Level HIE

Healthcare providers also participate in national networks:

  • Epic Care Everywhere: Connects Epic EHR systems
  • CareQuality: Nationwide interoperability framework
  • CommonWell: Health data sharing alliance
  • eHealth Exchange: Federal and private sector HIE

Contact your provider's Privacy Officer to opt out of these.
