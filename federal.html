Federal HIE Networks

Federal health information exchanges operate at the national level and may share health data across government healthcare systems. Learn about your opt-out options for federal HIE networks.

⚠️ Important Notice

Federal HIE opt-out processes may have different requirements and restrictions compared to state or private provider systems. Some federal healthcare benefits may be affected by opting out of information sharing.

Major Federal HIE Networks

Department of Veterans Affairs (VA)

Federal Healthcare System

VA Health Information Exchange

The VA operates its own electronic health record system and participates in data sharing with other healthcare providers to coordinate care for veterans.

Privacy Office Contact

VA Privacy Office

Phone: 1-844-4VA-FOIA (1-844-482-3642)

Email: vaprivacyofficer@va.gov

Website: VA Privacy Policy

Opt-Out Process

  • Contact your VA healthcare provider or facility's Release of Information office
  • Submit a written request to restrict sharing of your health information
  • Specify which types of information sharing you want to restrict
  • Note: Some restrictions may affect your ability to receive coordinated care

Important Considerations

  • VA may still share information for treatment, payment, and healthcare operations
  • Emergency situations may override some opt-out preferences
  • Certain VA benefits may require information sharing

Department of Defense (DoD)

Military Healthcare System

Military Health System (MHS)

The DoD operates TRICARE and military treatment facilities, with health information systems that may share data for military readiness and healthcare coordination.

Privacy Office Contact

DoD Privacy Office

Phone: 1-571-372-0461

Website: DoD Privacy Program

Opt-Out Process

  • Contact your military treatment facility's Privacy Officer
  • For TRICARE, contact your regional contractor
  • Submit requests in writing with specific restrictions desired
  • Active duty personnel may have limited opt-out options

Military-Specific Considerations

  • Active duty status may limit some privacy options
  • Mission-critical health information may be exempt from restrictions
  • Family members may have different opt-out rights than service members

eHealth Exchange

National HIE Network

Nationwide Health Information Network

A national network that enables secure health information exchange between federal agencies, states, and private healthcare organizations.

Network Information

eHealth Exchange

Website: eHealth Exchange

Participants: Federal agencies, state HIEs, healthcare organizations

Opt-Out Process

  • Contact your healthcare provider's privacy office
  • Request restrictions on participation in national HIE networks
  • Specify eHealth Exchange participation restrictions
  • May need separate requests for each participating organization

Indian Health Service (IHS)

Federal Healthcare System

Provides healthcare services to American Indians and Alaska Natives, with health information systems that may participate in broader HIE networks.

Opt-Out Process

  • Contact your local IHS facility's privacy officer
  • Submit written request for information sharing restrictions
  • Specify which networks or types of sharing to restrict
  • Consider impact on coordinated care with other IHS facilities

Federal vs. State HIE Differences

Federal HIE Systems

  • Governed by federal privacy regulations (HIPAA, Privacy Act)
  • May have national security or public health exemptions
  • Often involve multiple federal agencies
  • May affect federal healthcare benefits
  • Standardized across all states

State HIE Systems

  • Governed by state privacy laws (may be more restrictive)
  • Vary significantly by state
  • Typically involve state and local healthcare providers
  • May affect state-funded healthcare programs
  • Often have clearer opt-out processes

General Steps for Federal HIE Opt-Out

1

Identify Your Federal Healthcare Providers

Determine which federal healthcare systems you receive care from (VA, DoD/TRICARE, IHS, etc.)

2

Contact Privacy Offices

Reach out to each system's privacy office or your facility's privacy officer

3

Submit Written Requests

Provide detailed, written requests specifying which information sharing to restrict

4

Understand Limitations

Be aware of restrictions that may still apply due to federal regulations or benefit requirements

5

Get Written Confirmation

Request written confirmation of your opt-out status and any limitations
